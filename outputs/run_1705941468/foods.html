The American Diet: Missing Nutrients, the Power of Plant-Based Food, and Culinary Innovations

22/01/2024

Despite the increasing emphasis on healthy eating among Americans, the standard American diet is lacking in four critical nutrients, posing a major public health concern. According to the Dietary Guidelines for Americans, a government guide outlining what to consume to meet nutrient needs, promote health and prevent disease, Americans are deficient in potassium, calcium, and vitamin D. These nutrients are essential for optimal health, and their deficiency could lead to serious health problems.

Potassium, one of the nutrients largely missing in the American diet, can be found in various fruits, vegetables, lentils, kidney beans, and animal products such as milk, yogurt, chicken, and salmon. Similarly, calcium-rich foods include dairy products, canned salmon, sardines, and dark leafy greens like spinach and kale. Most of the vitamin D in American diets comes from fortified dairy products and ready-to-eat cereals. This transition towards a diet rich in these foods can help combat the deficiencies seen in the American diet.

In a recent study, it was found that adopting a plant-based diet can have significant benefits in preventing chronic illnesses, particularly in women as they age. The research advocated for the majority of protein intake to come from plant sources, supplemented with a small amount of animal protein. The benefits of plant-based proteins were evident in lowered 'bad' cholesterol (LDL cholesterol), blood pressure, and insulin sensitivity. In contrast, consuming animal protein was linked to higher levels.

In the realm of food innovation, strides are being made to address these concerns. For instance, a new prototype for cultivated eel has been developed by Forsea. This innovation offers a potentially sustainable alternative to overfishing, addressing both environmental and dietary concerns. Similarly, in the home cooking sector, new mealtime convenience solutions are emerging. These products are tailored to minimize a home chef’s time in the kitchen while ensuring nutrient-rich meals, aligning with the growing emphasis on sustainability and convenience in the food industry.

Exploring global culinary landscapes can also provide inspiration for healthier eating. For example, Trinidad's culinary scene offers a vibrant blend of street food culture, seafood, meat and vegetarian dishes, pepper sauces, curries and seasonings, all of which reflect a multi-ethnic celebratory food tradition. Such diverse food cultures can potentially inspire Americans to diversify their diets, thereby addressing nutrient deficiencies and ultimately improving public health.
