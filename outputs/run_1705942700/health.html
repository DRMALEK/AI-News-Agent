The Holistic Approach to Health: Prioritising Self-Care and Physical Activity

22/01/2024

In the wake of the global health crisis, the importance of self-care and overall wellness has emerged as a top priority. Recent articles from Bellarine Times and Surf Coast Times emphasise the need to prioritise self-care in 2024, advocating for a holistic approach that benefits both physical and mental well-being. Self-care isn't merely about indulging in short-term fixes like holidays or spa days, but about building a consistent, sustainable routine that promotes a positive mindset and lifestyle. This might involve establishing a regular sleep schedule, incorporating balanced meals into your diet, or setting aside time each day for relaxation and mindfulness practices.

The Australian Institute of Health and Welfare found that more than 40% of Australians between the ages of 16 and 85 had experienced poor mental health at some point in their lives, with one in every five people having dealt with this in the past year. To thrive, it's important to nurture all aspects of health, including mental, emotional, physical, environmental, spiritual, recreational, and social. A disruption in any of these factors can lead to a domino effect on overall well-being. For instance, physical health issues can impact mental and emotional health, while social isolation can contribute to feelings of stress and anxiety.

Physical activity is also a critical element of maintaining good health. Research from the University of Jyväskylä in Finland, featured in The News International, suggests that regular medium- to high-intensity physical activity can help mitigate the health risks associated with prolonged sitting, common in today's office-based work culture. Simple habits like pacing a few times every hour, taking a walk while on a call, or incorporating regular stretch breaks into your workday can make a significant difference.

The wellness culture is becoming polarised, as reported by Women's Health UK. On one hand, there are individuals who prefer a data-driven approach, meticulously monitoring their health metrics using smart devices and health apps. This method offers a detailed understanding of one's health status and progress, but can also lead to stress or obsession over numbers. On the other hand, there are those who advocate for a more intuitive, feelings-based method, paying close attention to their body's signals and responding accordingly. This approach promotes a strong sense of body awareness and self-trust, but might lack the precision and objectivity provided by data. This division underlines the importance of finding a balanced, individualised approach to health and wellness that suits one's personal needs and preferences.

Looking to the future, the World Health Organisation stresses the need for global cooperation and preparation in anticipation of potential future pandemics. These global health threats underline the importance of individual health and wellness, as robust personal health can contribute to stronger community resilience. By prioritising our own self-care and physical health, each of us can play a part in fostering global health and wellness.
