The Current State of Nutritional Health and Sustainable Food Practices

22/01/2024

A significant portion of U.S. adults prioritize health-conscious dietary habits, yet studies indicate deficiencies in certain essential nutrients within the standard American diet. These findings are based on the Dietary Guidelines for Americans, a federal guide aimed at promoting health and preventing disease through nutrient needs. The guide identifies four critical nutrients - potassium, calcium, and vitamin D among them - that are consumed in such low quantities that it has become a public health concern.

Potassium, calcium, and vitamin D are considered critical nutrients due to their integral roles in maintaining various bodily functions. Potassium, primarily found in fruits, vegetables, lentils, kidney beans, and animal products like milk, yogurt, chicken, and salmon, assists in regulating fluid balance, nerve signals, and muscle contractions. Calcium, which is rich in dairy products, canned fish, and dark leafy greens, is vital for bone health and proper functioning of the heart, muscles, and nerves. Vitamin D, largely obtained from fortified dairy products and ready-to-eat cereals, is crucial for calcium absorption, immune function, and reduction of inflammation.

In other food-related developments, culinary innovation continues to inspire the creation of delicious and nutritious meals. The incorporation of fried food in the diet during the rainy, grey winter season, for instance, can uplift moods and offer comfort. Meanwhile, with sustainability in mind, companies are developing prototypes for cultivated eel and other seafood products, marking a significant stride in the field of alternative proteins.

Celebrities such as Emily Ratajkwoski are acknowledging the role of diet in maintaining their physique. Ratajkwoski credits home-cooked meals for her toned body, emphasizing the control it provides over the intake of salt, sugar, and other dietary components. This underscores the benefits of home cooking in managing dietary components for overall health.

On a global scale, there is a growing interest in returning to the cultivation of traditional crops to combat food insecurity. In Africa, for instance, following decades of American policies promoting the growth of staple grains like maize, there is a shift towards traditional crops like cowpeas, cassava, and a range of millets. This shift is driven by the need to diversify food sources, increase resilience to climate shocks, and mitigate the rising costs and political instability associated with food insecurity. If successful, this shift could prove 'genuinely revolutionary'.
