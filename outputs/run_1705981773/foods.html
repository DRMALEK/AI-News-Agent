Foods: What We Choose, How We Benefit and What We Lack

22/01/2024

In our evolving understanding and approach to food, various factors come into play. From personal health objectives, dietary restrictions to ethical considerations around sourcing, our food choices are becoming increasingly nuanced. For instance, many households are now adhering to ethical dietary practices such as the halal diet, focused on the ethical treatment of animals. Others are gravitating towards meal prepping using nutrient-dense foods like organic tofu, salmon, and spinach-and-cheese ravioli, as observed in the shopping patterns of a frequent customer at Costco.

Shifting gears to the consumers' perspective, a recent survey from Chain Store Age uncovered some interesting insights. It was found that 'guaranteed fresh' and 'good for your health' top the list of attributes consumers seek when purchasing food, followed by 'nutritional value' and 'gives you energy'. Simultaneously, as household budgets tighten, consumers are re-evaluating their spending habits. While spending on certain categories is expected to decrease, essentials like food and healthcare remain unaffected.

Despite the growing emphasis on healthier eating, there's a glaring gap in the standard American diet - a deficiency in key nutrients. As per the Dietary Guidelines for Americans, four critical nutrients – potassium, calcium, and vitamin D, are lacking in most American diets. These nutrients can be primarily found in fruits, vegetables, lentils, dairy products, and animal products such as chicken and salmon. This highlights the importance of a balanced diet and the need for continued education and awareness in this regard.

In addition to health and nutrition, food also serves as a celebration of cultural diversity and variety. Nowhere is this more evident than in the vibrant food culture of Trinidad. From street food and seafood to pepper sauces, curries, and creole cuisine - the island's food scene is a melting pot of African, Spanish, and Indigenous influences. This culinary richness has caught the attention of global food bloggers and Michelin chefs alike.

One of the ways individuals are taking control of their diets is through home cooking. Emily Ratajkwoski, a renowned model, credits her cooking practice for helping her monitor the amount of salt and sugar in her diet. This practice, coupled with informed food choices and a focus on nutrient-rich foods, can significantly enhance individual and community health.
