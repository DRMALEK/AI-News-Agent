Reimagining Our Diets: Nutrition Insights and Innovations

22/01/2024

Nutritional Deficiencies in American Diets: Even as a significant number of U.S. adults consider healthy eating a priority, the standard American diet is found wanting in certain critical nutrients. The Dietary Guidelines for Americans have identified potassium, calcium, and vitamin D as some of the nutrients that most Americans consume in worryingly low quantities. These deficiencies, according to the federal government's guide, are concerning enough to be viewed as a 'public health concern'. Foods such as fruits (especially dried ones), vegetables, lentils, kidney beans, dairy products, and certain types of fish are excellent sources of these nutrients.

Fried Food - A Winter Delight: Meanwhile, despite the cold and dreary weather, food enthusiasts can find warmth and joy in various culinary delights. Fried food, for instance, has been suggested as a way to perk up spirits during the long winter months. Recipes like rice noodle-crusted fried shrimp and crab rangoon with plum sauce, inspired by the East Bay institution, Trader Vic’s, can be a delightful centerpiece at any winter gathering.

Plant-based Protein and Women's Health: In other food-related news, a recent study by The American Journal of Clinical Nutrition has revealed that consuming plant-based protein can significantly reduce the odds of women developing chronic illnesses as they age. Compared to animal proteins, plant proteins were found to lower 'bad' cholesterol, blood pressure, and insulin sensitivity. The study, conducted by researchers at Tufts University, found that women who consume more protein from fruits, vegetables, legumes, and beans were less likely to develop diabetes, cancer, or heart disease.

Innovation in Food Technology - Cultivated Seafood: On the technological front, cultivated seafood is making waves in the food industry. Forsea, a company focusing on this innovative technology, has developed a cultivated eel prototype. This development is part of the broader trend of cultured meat technology, which produces genuine animal meat by cultivating animal cells, primarily focusing on land-based species.

Revolutionizing Agriculture with Traditional Crops: Finally, a 'revolutionary' approach to feeding the world is in the works, involving a return to traditional crops. Instead of focusing solely on staple grains like maize, the trend is shifting towards traditional crops like cowpeas, cassava, and a variety of millets. As an example, in certain regions of Africa, there has been a successful transition back to these crops, potentially addressing the rising food insecurity worsened by climate shocks and rising costs.
