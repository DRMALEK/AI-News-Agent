The Holistic Approach to Health: Prioritizing Self-Care and Lifestyle Tweaks for a Longer Life

22/01/2024

As we step further into 2024, the emphasis on self-care and maintaining a balanced lifestyle is more pertinent than ever. The Australian Institute of Health and Welfare found that more than 40% of Australians between the ages of 16 and 85 had experienced poor mental health at some point, highlighting the need for a holistic approach to self-care. This includes taking care of one's mental, emotional, physical, environmental, spiritual, recreational, and social health. For example, one could adopt practices such as meditating daily for mental health, regular exercise for physical health, and spending time in nature for environmental health.

While quick fixes such as spa days or holidays provide momentary relief, they are not sustainable solutions for long-term mental and physical well-being. Instead, building personal care practices into our daily routines is key to achieving lasting benefits and maintaining a positive mindset. This can range from cutting back on alcohol to taking regular breaks from workstations and incorporating medium- to high-intensity physical activity into one's schedule. For instance, taking five-minute breaks every hour during the workday can help prevent digital eye strain and improve mental focus.

The health space is not immune to polarization, with some meticulously tracking their health metrics and others preferring a more intuitive approach. However, striking a balance between these two extremes is crucial. On one hand, making use of data and tracking abilities can provide valuable insights into one's health progress. On the other hand, an intuitive approach emphasizes the importance of listening to one's body and mental state, providing a more personalized and flexible approach to health management.

Offering a more tangible approach, longevity experts suggest five simple lifestyle tweaks to reach the age of 100. These include consuming more plants and oily fish while reducing processed meat intake, finding a balanced exercise regimen, maintaining social contact, focusing on good sleep, and managing stress. The anti-inflammatory properties of plants and fish can reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease, while social contact can boost immune function and reduce stress levels. Research from the American Heart Association supports these claims, emphasizing the importance of a healthy diet and regular exercise in reducing cardiovascular disease risk.

In the age of digital exposure, taking care of our eyes is equally important. Digital eye strain is a common issue that arises from continuous exposure to screens without adequate breaks. It's a reminder that in our quest for a healthier lifestyle, it's important to consider all aspects of our health, including those that may seem minor in the larger scheme of things. A study published in the Journal of Medical Internet Research found that taking regular breaks from screen time can significantly reduce the risk of digital eye strain.
