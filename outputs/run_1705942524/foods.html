A Deep Dive into America’s Dietary Gaps and Food Innovations

22/01/2024

Despite the increasing awareness and consideration of healthy eating among U.S. adults, the standard American diet seems to be falling short on several key nutrients. According to recent findings, the Dietary Guidelines for Americans, a guide published every five years by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services and the U.S. Department of Agriculture, to help citizens meet nutrient requirements and promote health while preventing disease, identified four vital nutrients that are woefully lacking in American diets. These are potassium, calcium, vitamin D, and dietary fiber.

Potassium, a crucial nutrient, is primarily found in fruits, such as dried fruit like raisins, prunes, and apricots. Other rich sources include vegetables like potatoes, tomatoes, and broccoli, as well as lentils, kidney beans, and animal products like milk, yogurt, chicken, and salmon. Calcium, another essential nutrient, is abundant in dairy products such as milk, yogurt, and cheese, canned salmon, sardines, and dark leafy greens like spinach and kale. Vitamin D is fortified in many foods such as dairy products including milk and yogurt and ready-to-eat cereals, which provide most of the vitamin D in American diets.

Moving from nutrient deficiencies to culinary innovations, the food industry is adapting to the changing times. For instance, to beat the dreary winter weather, comforting food options are being innovated. Fried food, has been making waves, with delicacies such as rice noodle-crusted 'coco' fried shrimp and crab Rangoon with plum sauce being used to perk up a rainy, grey winter. These foods not only offer warmth and comfort but also provide an opportunity for social gatherings.

In the realm of meat alternatives, a breakthrough has been achieved with the development of a new prototype for cultivated eel. This innovative method uses cultured meat technology, whereby animal meat is produced by cultivating animal cells. So far, this technology has mostly been applied to land-based species, but the advancement into seafood is a promising step toward sustainable and ethical food production.

Lastly, let's consider the personal experiences of people who prioritize balanced diets for maintaining their health and wellness. Take, for instance, Emily Ratajkwoski, a world-renowned model. She attributes her health and physique to a balanced diet and emphasizes the importance of knowing what goes into one's body. Her example underscores the vital role that balanced diets play in overall health and wellness, a theme that resonates in the broader discussion about food, nutrition, and health.
