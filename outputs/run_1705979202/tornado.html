These strange curls of snow, formed by an intense band of lake-effect snow, swept across northeast Illinois and northwest Indiana last Friday, dropping nearly three feet of snow in a narrow swath only a few miles wide. This mesmerizing spectacle of converging winds creating meso-vortices represents a stark reminder of the immense power of nature. The greater the temperature difference between the air aloft and waters below, the heavier the resulting snows, underlining the intricate relationship between atmospheric conditions and meteorological events.
